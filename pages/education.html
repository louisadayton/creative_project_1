SEA TURTLE CONSERVATION

Why are sea turtles endangered?

The IUCN has identified five major contributers to sea turtle endangerment:
  • Fisheries: Fishing can negatively impact the food web, destroy sea turtle habitats, and kill sea turtles that get entangled in nets.
  • Hunting: Sea turtles and their eggs are hunted throughout the world.
  • Development: Development such as seafloor dredging and coastal construction alters and even destroys sea turtle habitats and nesting areas.
  • Pollution: Sea turtles can die from ingesting or being tangled in pollutants. Light pollution disrupts nesting patterns and leads baby sea turtles away from the sea.
  • Climate Change: Climate change will cause damage to sea turtle habitats and populations.

Sources: Caribbean Conservation Corporation and Todd Steiner, Sea Turtle Restoration Project

Do Your Part

Pollutants are one of the main causes of preventable sea turtle deaths. Here are some things you can do to help protect our flippery friends:
  • Participate in the Highland Herpetarium's annual beach cleanup. Every summer, we get together for a day of family-friendly service. Specific dates and times will be posted on our Events Page.
  • Clean up after yourself. Do your part to leave the beach cleaner than when you came. Look out for debris that could entangle or be eaten by sea turtles. Fill in holes and kick down sand castles before you leave, as they can become obstacles for baby sea turtle trying to reach the ocean.
  • Turn off or take down lights that are visible from the ocean. Inland lights can disorient baby sea turtles, and they can die if they crawl away from the ocean.
  • Raise awareness. Join a campain in your city to educate others on sea turtle conservation. Write to your representatives in Congress to influence legislation that influences sea habitats.
